The Batch

The year the
dead came home

The harvest began on the morning of October 25th, 2022, in the fields of Benito Ruiz and his son — around 150 espadín agave, cut by hand before the afternoon heat.

Roasted in an earthen pit over wood fire, fermented on wild yeast, and twice-distilled in copper stills sealed with local barro clay by Maestro Mezcalero Tomás Mendéz. The final run finished on November 23rd, rested into 1,250 numbered bottles.

It was the first Día de los Muertos after three years of lockdown — the year Oaxaca could welcome its dead home again. That spirit is what you are holding.
