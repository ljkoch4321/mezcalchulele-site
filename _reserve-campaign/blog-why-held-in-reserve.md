# Why We Hold Our Mezcal in Reserve

People ask us a fair question: if the bottles exist, why can't they just buy one?

The honest answer is that we'd rather they didn't — not yet, and not from a cart.

## Made a little, made once

We don't run a production line. Each Chulele release is a single batch, tied to one harvest and one season in Oaxaca. There is no "more" to order. When a batch is finished, the count is fixed forever — 1,250 bottles of Día de los Muertos 2022, and no more, ever.

When something is that finite, selling it as fast as possible is the wrong instinct. A great single batch deserves to find the right homes, not just the fastest checkouts.

## What "held in reserve" means

Held in reserve is not sold out. Sold out is an accident — you wanted to sell and ran out. Reserve is a choice. We are keeping these bottles back, deliberately, and releasing them by allocation:

- The batch is not listed for open purchase.
- We open small windows, by invitation, to people on the reserve list.
- The rest waits — for the right moment, the right table, the next harvest.

It's closer to how a small grower handles a great vintage than how a brand handles inventory.

## Why this is better for the mezcal — and for you

A bottle that took a year of someone's life to make should not be treated like a commodity. Holding it in reserve does three things:

1. **It protects the work.** The maestro's batch isn't dumped into the market and discounted; it's placed.
2. **It keeps the story intact.** Every bottle that leaves goes to someone who knows what they're holding.
3. **It rewards patience over speed.** The people who care get first access — not the fastest bots or the biggest carts.

We're not interested in being everywhere. We're interested in being worth finding.

## How to get one

There's no buy button to hunt for. [Join the reserve list](/reserve/) and leave your name. When a bottle is meant for you — or when the next batch comes down from the hills — we write to you first.

That's the whole offer. Quiet, finite, and by hand. The way the mezcal was made.
