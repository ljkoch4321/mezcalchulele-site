# Día de los Muertos in a Bottle: The 2022 Chulele Reserve

Some batches are just a date on a calendar. This one was a homecoming.

## The year the dead came home

The 2022 Día de los Muertos was the first after three years of lockdown — the first time Oaxaca could gather, build its altars, and welcome its dead back without restraint. The air over Santiago Matatlán was thick with marigolds, cock's-comb flowers, food on the fire, and copal smoke. We made a mezcal to hold that moment.

Our maestro mezcalero, Tomás Mendéz, began the harvest on the morning of October 25th, cutting roughly 150 espadín agave by hand in the fields of Benito Ruiz and his son — early, before the heat. Enough for one thousand litres. One batch.

## How it was made

Nothing here was rushed:

- **Roasted** in an earthen pit over wood fire, for days, until the agave turned dark and sweet.
- **Fermented** in the open air, on wild yeast — no shortcuts, no additives.
- **Distilled twice** in copper stills sealed with local *barro* clay, the final run finished on November 23rd.
- **Bottled** into 1,250 numbered bottles. Espadín Joven, 51.3% ABV, 100% agave.

The result has a bold nose and flavour that still manages to feel gentle: ripe fruit, cinnamon, a soft smoke, a finish of roasted plantain.

## Why you can't simply buy it

The batch is finished. There will never be another Día de los Muertos 2022 — that's what single batch means. Rather than sell the last of it on demand, we [hold it in reserve](/reserve/) and release by allocation, so each bottle finds the right hands.

You can [read the full story of this batch](/product/dia-de-muertos-2022/), and if it speaks to you, [join the reserve list](/reserve/). When a bottle is meant for you, we'll be in touch.
