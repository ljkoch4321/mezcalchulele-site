Tasting Notes

Aspect

Colorless. Served at 20°C in a flute glass.

Aroma

Ripe fruit, cinnamon, and a gentle smokiness.

Palate

Balanced, slightly dry and astringent, with soft citrus — mandarin and orange peel.

Finish

A warm aftertaste of roasted plantain.

The story in the bottle

The harvest began on the morning of October 25th, 2022, in the fields of Benito Ruiz and his son — roughly 150 espadín plants cut by hand before the afternoon heat, enough for 1,000 litres. Roasted in an earthen oven, distilled twice in copper stills sealed with barro clay, and rested into 1,250 numbered bottles.

This was the year Oaxaca emerged from lockdown and welcomed its dead home after three long years. That spirit is what you are asking to hold.

Tomás Mendéz Hernández
Maestro Mezcalero
